## About

This glossary serves as a reference for key terms used in managing materials, inventory, and transactions within the system. It provides clear definitions for technical and operational concepts related to procurement, stock management, reporting, and distribution. The terms included cover various modules and functions, ensuring consistency in communication across different stakeholders, including health offices, warehouses, and suppliers.

By standardising terminology, this glossary helps users navigate the system more effectively, understand key processes, and maintain data accuracy when managing materials, tracking transactions, and generating reports. It is designed to support decision-making and ensure seamless coordination between entities at different levels, from local health facilities to central distribution units.

## Glossary

### A 

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Activity | A specific task or operation carried out on the Program based on a real-life activity. |
| Activity process, bottom up | A workflow approach where decisions and material process flow from lower levels (e.g., Public Health Centre) to higher levels (e.g., Ministry of Health or Material Manufacturer). For detailed information on the hierarchical Health Care Entity level, please visit the Ministry of Health Healthcare System Overview page. |
| Activity process, top down | A workflow approach where decisions and directives originate from higher levels and are implemented at lower levels |
| Allocated Stock | Stock that has been reserved or allocated for specific orders or transactions and is no longer available for other orders or transactions. |
| Available Stock | The total material that is still available and has not been ordered or transacted by any entity. |

---

### B

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Buffer Province | A stock reserve is maintained at the provincial level to ensure a steady supply of materials to lower-level entities, mitigating demand fluctuations and replenishment delays. This buffer helps ensure that material stock will not be insufficient at an entity based on its calculated distribution or consumption level. |
| Budget Source | The designated funding allocation is used to finance material purchases, ensuring traceability and compliance with financial planning. |

---

### C

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Consumption Customer | A child entity that directly uses or administers materials, such as a healthcare facility or clinic. This setting is used for the Transaction process. |
| Central Buffer | A stock reserve is maintained at the national or central level to support provincial and regional distribution, ensuring availability and preventing shortages across multiple locations. This buffer is designed to avoid supply disruptions by maintaining an adequate reserve based on projected needs and historical consumption patterns. |
| Companion Material | A supportive material that is used alongside another material to enhance or facilitate its application. |
| Closing Stock History | The quantity of materials after transactions or orders have been processed. |
| Contract Number | A unique identifier assigned to a contractual agreement between entities, typically used for procurement, ordering, or funding purposes. |
| Consumption Rate | The frequency at which materials are used over a specific time. |
| Central Distribution Order | A directive from a central authority (Ministry of Health or Material Manufacturer) to distribute materials to various entities, ensuring stock availability at different levels. |

---

### D

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Delivery Order Number | A unique identifier assigned to a delivery order issued by the central distribution system, used to track and manage the shipment of materials to various entities. |
| Distribution Order | An order is issued to allocate and distribute materials from one entity to another within the supply chain. |
| Doses | The specific quantity of a vaccine or medication required for administration |
| Direct Purchase | A transaction reason that allows an entity to purchase materials directly using allocated funds. This process opens a form option to specify the source of funds from a budget source. |
| Distribution Customer | A child entity from the parent's lower-level entity that receives materials as part of the distribution process. This setting is used for the Order process. |

---

### E

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Entity | The organization that handles health activities, like the Ministry of Health, drug factories, health offices, health centers, or other healthcare facilities. They help manage vaccines and medical supplies. |
| Entity Tag | A label or identifier used to categorise and differentiate entities within the system. |
| Entity Type | A classification of entities based on their level, such as Province, City, Health Office, or Central (Ministry of Health or Material's Manufacturer). |

---

### H

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Healthcare Entity Level | A classification of healthcare organizations, from the national health office down to local health facilities, that manage the distribution and delivery of vaccines and medical supplies within a structured supply chain system. For more information, please visit Ministry of Health Healthcare System Overview page. |

---

### I

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| In-transit stock | The quantity of material that is on shipment between entities, no longer at the vendor or customer, and in the process of being delivered. |
| is vendor | A status indicating whether an entity serves as a supplier of materials. |

---

### K

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| KFA | Stands for Kamus Farmasi dan Alkes is a classification system used to categorise materials based on standardised criteria. |

---

### M

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Max Qty | The recommended maximum allowable quantity of materials that can be stored to prevent overstocking and wastage. |
| Medical Consumable | Single-use or limited-use healthcare materials, such as gloves, surgical masks, and lancets, that are consumed during medical procedures. |
| Manage by Batch | A process where materials are tracked and managed based on batch numbers to ensure quality and expiration control. |
| Material Level | The hierarchical categorisation of materials, such as Active Substance (KFA 92), Trademark (KFA 93), or Non-Hierarchical material. |
| Material Type | A classification of materials based on their characteristics, such as vaccines, medical supplies, or equipment |
| Max Temperature | The highest allowable temperature at which materials must be stored to prevent spoilage or reduced efficacy. |
| Min Qty | The recommended minimum quantity of materials that must be maintained in stock to avoid shortages and ensure operational continuity. |
| Min Temperature | The lowest allowable temperature at which materials must be stored to maintain their effectiveness and prevent degradation of their quality. |

---

### N

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Non-Medical Consumable | Disposable operational items, such as insecticide, alcohol swabs, and bed nets. |

---

### O

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Opening Stock History | The quantity of materials is determined before any transaction or order occurs. |
| Order Status | The current stage of an order within the system tracks the progress of an order from creation to fulfilment. For detailed information on the order status, please visit the Order Document page. |

---

### P

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Product Template | The primary ingredient in a vaccine or medication responsible for its intended effect is classified as KFA 92. |
| Product Variant | The registered brand name under which a material is sold, classified as KFA 93 |
| Program | Structured health initiative designed to address specific diseases or health conditions by managing and distributing critical resources, such as vaccines, drugs, and medical supplies. |

---

### R

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Receiver | An entity that receives materials or products from the order or transaction process within the system |
| Retailer Price | The price at which an entity purchases materials from a vendor or supplier. |
| Return Order | An order is created to return unused or defective materials to the supplier or another entity. |
| Request Order | An order initiated by an entity to request materials from a higher-level entity or supplier. |
| Role | A designated responsibility and privilege assigned to a user within the system. |
| Remaining Stock | The total quantity of material currently owned and physically available within an entity. |


---

### s

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Sender | An entity responsible for supplying materials or products within the ordering or transaction process. |

### T

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Temperature Sensitive | A defined term, whether a material is sensitive to a specific temperature, where exposure beyond the recommended range may degrade its quality. |
